# What is a Robot?

A **robot** is a programmable machine capable of **sensing**, **planning**, and **acting** in its environment.

## The Sense-Plan-Act Cycle

Every robot operates on a fundamental loop:

1. **Sense** — Perceive the environment through sensors (cameras, LIDAR, touch)
2. **Plan** — Process sensor data and decide what to do next
3. **Act** — Execute the plan through actuators (motors, grippers)

$$
\text{Robot} = \text{Sensors} + \text{Computation} + \text{Actuators}
$$

## Degrees of Freedom (DoF)

The **degrees of freedom** of a robot define how many independent ways it can move.

- A robot arm with 6 joints has **6 DoF**
- A mobile robot on a plane has **3 DoF** ($x$, $y$, $\theta$)
- The human arm has approximately **7 DoF**

$$
\text{DoF} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} f_i
$$

where $f_i$ is the freedom of the $i$-th joint.

## Open-Loop vs Closed-Loop Control

| Type | Feedback? | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Open-loop** | No | Toaster timer |
| **Closed-loop** | Yes | Thermostat |

In robotics, we almost always use **closed-loop (feedback) control** because the real world is unpredictable.

## Key Takeaway

> A robot is not defined by how it looks, but by what it can do: sense, plan, and act.
