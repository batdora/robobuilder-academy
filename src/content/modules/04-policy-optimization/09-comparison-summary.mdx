# Policy Optimization: The Full Picture

This lesson synthesizes everything from the module. We compare all algorithms, understand when to use which, connect back to value-based methods, and review the key formulas you need to know.

## The Evolution of Policy Gradient Methods

The development from REINFORCE to PPO follows a clear arc of **reducing variance** and **improving stability**:

```
REINFORCE (1992)
  |-- High variance, simple
  v
+ Baseline Subtraction
  |-- Reduced variance, still noisy
  v
+ Temporal Structure (reward-to-go)
  |-- Better credit assignment
  v
Actor-Critic / A2C (bootstrapping)
  |-- Lower variance via learned V(s)
  v
+ GAE (bias-variance tradeoff)
  |-- Tunable lambda parameter
  v
Natural Policy Gradient
  |-- Parameterization-invariant updates
  v
TRPO (2015)
  |-- Guaranteed improvement via KL constraint
  v
PPO (2017)
  |-- Same stability, much simpler
```

## Master Comparison Table

| Feature | REINFORCE | A2C | TRPO | PPO |
|---------|-----------|-----|------|-----|
| **Type** | Monte Carlo PG | Actor-Critic | Trust Region | Clipped Surrogate |
| **Advantage Estimate** | $G_t - b$ | $\delta_t$ or $n$-step | GAE | GAE |
| **Value Function** | Optional baseline | Required (critic) | Required | Required |
| **Optimization** | SGD | SGD | CG + line search | Adam/SGD |
| **Trust Region** | None | None | KL constraint | Implicit (clipping) |
| **Update Frequency** | End of episode | Every step or $n$-step | Batch | Batch, multi-epoch |
| **Variance** | Very high | Low-medium | Low | Low |
| **Bias** | None (MC) | Some (bootstrap) | Some (GAE) | Some (GAE) |
| **Implementation** | Simple | Moderate | Complex | Simple |
| **Sample Efficiency** | Low | Medium | Medium-high | Medium-high |
| **Stability** | Unstable | Moderate | Very stable | Very stable |
| **Monotonic Improvement** | No | No | Theoretically yes | Empirically yes |

## Key Formulas Summary

### 1. Policy Gradient Theorem (Likelihood Ratio)

$$
\nabla_\theta U(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{\tau \sim \pi_\theta}\left[\sum_{t=0}^{H} \nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta(u_t | s_t) \cdot R(\tau)\right]
$$

### 2. REINFORCE with Baseline

$$
\hat{g} = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}\sum_{t=0}^{H-1} \nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta(u_t^{(i)} | s_t^{(i)}) \left(\sum_{k=t}^{H-1} R(s_k^{(i)}, u_k^{(i)}) - b(s_t^{(i)})\right)
$$

### 3. Advantage Function

$$
A^\pi(s, a) = Q^\pi(s, a) - V^\pi(s)
$$

### 4. TD Error (1-step Advantage Estimate)

$$
\delta_t = r_t + \gamma V(s_{t+1}) - V(s_t)
$$

### 5. GAE($\gamma$, $\lambda$)

$$
\hat{A}_t^{GAE} = \sum_{l=0}^{\infty}(\gamma\lambda)^l \delta_{t+l}
$$

Recursive: $\hat{A}_t = \delta_t + \gamma\lambda\hat{A}_{t+1}$

### 6. Surrogate Loss (TRPO/PPO)

$$
L(\pi) = \mathbb{E}_{\pi_{\text{old}}}\left[\frac{\pi(a|s)}{\pi_{\text{old}}(a|s)} A^{\pi_{\text{old}}}(s, a)\right]
$$

### 7. TRPO Constraint

$$
\max_\pi L(\pi) \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \mathbb{E}\left[D_{KL}(\pi_{\text{old}} \| \pi)\right] \leq \epsilon
$$

### 8. PPO Clipped Objective

$$
L^{CLIP}(\theta) = \hat{\mathbb{E}}_t\left[\min\left(r_t(\theta)\hat{A}_t, \;\text{clip}(r_t(\theta), 1-\epsilon, 1+\epsilon)\hat{A}_t\right)\right]
$$

where $r_t(\theta) = \frac{\pi_\theta(a_t|s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}(a_t|s_t)}$

### 9. Natural Gradient

$$
\tilde{\nabla}_\theta J(\theta) = F(\theta)^{-1} \nabla_\theta J(\theta)
$$

where $F(\theta) = \mathbb{E}\left[\nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta(a|s) \nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta(a|s)^\top\right]$

### 10. KL Divergence (Dynamics Cancel)

$$
D_{KL}(\pi_\theta \| \pi_{\theta'}) \approx \frac{1}{M}\sum_{(s,u)} \log \frac{\pi_\theta(u|s)}{\pi_{\theta'}(u|s)}
$$

## When to Use Which Method

### Use REINFORCE when:
- You need a simple baseline implementation
- The environment has short episodes
- You are prototyping or teaching

### Use A2C when:
- You need online (per-step) updates
- Episodes are long or infinite-horizon
- You want moderate sample efficiency with simple code

### Use TRPO when:
- You need theoretical guarantees of improvement
- You can afford the computational overhead
- The task is sensitive to policy collapse

### Use PPO when:
- You want the best general-purpose algorithm
- You need something that "just works"
- You want to leverage GPU-optimized SGD
- You are scaling to large-scale problems (games, robotics, LLM alignment)

**In practice, PPO is the default choice** for most applications due to its simplicity, stability, and strong empirical performance.

## Connection to Value-Based Methods

Policy gradient and value-based methods are not mutually exclusive:

| Approach | What is Learned | Policy Extraction |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Value-based (DQN) | $Q(s,a)$ | $\arg\max_a Q(s,a)$ |
| Policy-based (REINFORCE) | $\pi_\theta(a|s)$ | Direct |
| Actor-Critic (A2C, PPO) | Both $\pi_\theta$ and $V_\phi$ | Direct (actor) |

Actor-Critic methods are a **bridge**: they use value-based ideas (learning $V$ or $Q$) to reduce the variance of policy gradient updates.

The **advantage** $A(s,a) = Q(s,a) - V(s)$ connects both worlds:
- From the value-based perspective: it measures relative action quality
- From the policy gradient perspective: it is the signal that tells us which actions to reinforce

## State of the Art Applications

Policy optimization methods (primarily PPO) have been used in:

- **Game playing:** OpenAI Five (Dota 2), Atari games, StarCraft
- **Robotics:** Locomotion (humanoid walking/running), dexterous manipulation (Rubik's cube), quadrotor control
- **Language models:** RLHF for ChatGPT, InstructGPT, Claude
- **Autonomous driving:** Policy optimization for planning
- **Chip design:** Google used RL for chip placement optimization
- **Soccer simulation:** Multi-agent learning for simulated soccer

## Key Takeaway

> This module covered the complete pipeline of policy optimization: from the likelihood ratio gradient, through variance reduction (baselines, temporal structure, GAE), to trust region methods (TRPO, PPO). The key insight is that we can compute model-free policy gradients using the log-derivative trick, and we can make these stable using trust regions. PPO, with its clipped surrogate objective, represents the current practical standard -- simple, stable, and effective across a vast range of applications.
