# Transformers

The **Transformer** (Vaswani et al., 2017, "Attention Is All You Need") replaces recurrence entirely with **self-attention**, enabling parallel processing and superior performance on sequence tasks. It has become the dominant architecture in NLP and is increasingly used in vision and robotics.

## Motivation: Why Replace RNNs?

RNNs process sequences **sequentially** -- $\mathbf{h}_t$ depends on $\mathbf{h}_{t-1}$. This means:
- Cannot parallelize computation across time steps
- Long-range dependencies still suffer despite LSTM/GRU
- Training is slow for long sequences

Transformers process all positions **in parallel** using self-attention.

## Self-Attention

In self-attention, each position in the sequence attends to **all other positions** (including itself). Given input embeddings $\mathbf{X} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times d}$, we compute:

$$
\mathbf{Q} = \mathbf{X}\mathbf{W}_Q, \quad \mathbf{K} = \mathbf{X}\mathbf{W}_K, \quad \mathbf{V} = \mathbf{X}\mathbf{W}_V
$$

where $\mathbf{W}_Q, \mathbf{W}_K \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d_k}$ and $\mathbf{W}_V \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d_v}$ are learned projection matrices.

$$
\text{Self-Attention}(\mathbf{Q}, \mathbf{K}, \mathbf{V}) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{K}^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)\mathbf{V}
$$

The matrix $\frac{\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{K}^T}{\sqrt{d_k}} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times T}$ computes pairwise similarity scores between all positions. Each output position is a weighted sum of **all** value vectors.

## Multi-Head Attention

Instead of performing a single attention function, Transformers use **multi-head attention** -- running $h$ attention operations in parallel with different learned projections:

$$
\text{head}_i = \text{Attention}(\mathbf{X}\mathbf{W}_Q^{(i)}, \mathbf{X}\mathbf{W}_K^{(i)}, \mathbf{X}\mathbf{W}_V^{(i)})
$$

$$
\text{MultiHead}(\mathbf{X}) = \text{Concat}(\text{head}_1, \ldots, \text{head}_h)\mathbf{W}_O
$$

where $\mathbf{W}_O \in \mathbb{R}^{hd_v \times d}$ combines the outputs. Typically $d_k = d_v = d / h$.

Each head can learn to attend to **different types of relationships** -- one head might focus on syntactic relations, another on semantic similarity, another on positional proximity.

## Positional Encoding

Self-attention is **permutation-invariant** -- it treats the input as a set, not a sequence. To inject position information, Transformers add **positional encodings** to the input embeddings:

$$
\text{PE}(pos, 2i) = \sin\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{2i/d}}\right)
$$

$$
\text{PE}(pos, 2i+1) = \cos\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{2i/d}}\right)
$$

where $pos$ is the position and $i$ is the dimension index. These sinusoidal functions allow the model to:
- Represent relative positions (since $\text{PE}(pos+k)$ can be expressed as a linear function of $\text{PE}(pos)$)
- Generalize to sequence lengths not seen during training

## Transformer Encoder Block

Each encoder layer consists of two sub-layers:

**1. Multi-Head Self-Attention:**

$$
\mathbf{Z} = \text{LayerNorm}(\mathbf{X} + \text{MultiHead}(\mathbf{X}))
$$

**2. Position-wise Feed-Forward Network (FFN):**

$$
\text{FFN}(\mathbf{z}) = \max(0, \mathbf{z}\mathbf{W}_1 + \mathbf{b}_1)\mathbf{W}_2 + \mathbf{b}_2
$$

$$
\text{Output} = \text{LayerNorm}(\mathbf{Z} + \text{FFN}(\mathbf{Z}))
$$

Key elements:
- **Residual connections** ($\mathbf{X} + \ldots$) prevent degradation in deep networks
- **Layer normalization** stabilizes training
- The FFN uses **ReLU** activation and is applied **independently** to each position

The encoder stacks $N$ such layers (typically $N = 6$).

## Transformer Decoder Block

The decoder has three sub-layers:

1. **Masked Multi-Head Self-Attention** -- prevents attending to future positions by masking:

$$
\text{Mask}_{ij} = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if } i \geq j \\ -\infty & \text{if } i < j \end{cases}
$$

This ensures autoregressive generation: position $t$ can only attend to positions $1, \ldots, t$.

2. **Cross-Attention** -- queries come from the decoder, keys and values from the encoder output:

$$
\text{CrossAttn}(\mathbf{Q}_{\text{dec}}, \mathbf{K}_{\text{enc}}, \mathbf{V}_{\text{enc}})
$$

3. **Feed-Forward Network** -- same as encoder

## The Full Transformer Architecture

$$
\text{Input} \xrightarrow{\text{Embedding + PE}} \underbrace{\text{Encoder} \times N}_{\text{Encode input}} \to \underbrace{\text{Decoder} \times N}_{\text{Generate output}} \xrightarrow{\text{Linear + Softmax}} \text{Output}
$$

## Computational Complexity

| Model | Time per Layer | Sequential Ops | Max Path Length |
|-------|---------------|----------------|-----------------|
| RNN | $O(T \cdot d^2)$ | $O(T)$ | $O(T)$ |
| Self-Attention | $O(T^2 \cdot d)$ | $O(1)$ | $O(1)$ |

Self-attention has $O(1)$ sequential operations (fully parallel) and $O(1)$ maximum path length between any two positions. The trade-off is $O(T^2)$ in sequence length for the attention matrix.

## Worked Example: Self-Attention for 3 Tokens

Given 3 token embeddings forming $\mathbf{Q}, \mathbf{K}, \mathbf{V} \in \mathbb{R}^{3 \times 2}$:

$$
\mathbf{Q} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}, \quad
\mathbf{K} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}
$$

**Attention scores** ($d_k = 2$):

$$
\frac{\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{K}^T}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \approx \begin{bmatrix} 0.71 & 0 & 0.71 \\ 0.71 & 0.71 & 0 \\ 1.41 & 0.71 & 0.71 \end{bmatrix}
$$

After softmax (row-wise), each row gives the attention weights for that position. Token 3 attends most strongly to Token 1 (score 1.41).

## Key Takeaway

> Transformers replace recurrence with self-attention, enabling parallel processing and direct connections between any two positions. Multi-head attention captures diverse relationships, positional encodings inject sequence order, and residual connections with layer normalization enable deep stacking. This architecture is the foundation of modern AI.
