# Attention Mechanism

The Seq2Seq bottleneck forces the encoder to compress an entire input sequence into a single vector. **Attention** (Bahdanau et al., 2014) eliminates this bottleneck by allowing the decoder to **look back at all encoder hidden states** and focus on the most relevant parts at each decoding step.

## Intuition

When translating a sentence, you do not read the entire sentence, memorize a single representation, and then translate. Instead, you **attend** to different parts of the source sentence as you generate each target word. Attention gives neural networks this same ability.

## Attention in Seq2Seq

Instead of using only the final encoder state, attention computes a **weighted combination** of all encoder hidden states at each decoder step.

Given encoder hidden states $\mathbf{h}_1^{\text{enc}}, \mathbf{h}_2^{\text{enc}}, \ldots, \mathbf{h}_T^{\text{enc}}$ and the current decoder state $\mathbf{h}_t^{\text{dec}}$:

**Step 1: Compute alignment scores** (how relevant is each encoder state?):

$$
e_{t,i} = \text{score}(\mathbf{h}_t^{\text{dec}}, \mathbf{h}_i^{\text{enc}})
$$

**Step 2: Normalize to get attention weights:**

$$
\alpha_{t,i} = \frac{\exp(e_{t,i})}{\sum_{j=1}^{T} \exp(e_{t,j})} = \text{softmax}(e_{t,i})
$$

**Step 3: Compute context vector** (weighted sum of encoder states):

$$
\mathbf{c}_t = \sum_{i=1}^{T} \alpha_{t,i} \mathbf{h}_i^{\text{enc}}
$$

**Step 4: Use context vector in decoder:**

$$
\mathbf{h}_t^{\text{dec}} = f(\mathbf{h}_{t-1}^{\text{dec}}, \mathbf{y}_{t-1}, \mathbf{c}_t)
$$

The context vector $\mathbf{c}_t$ is **different at each decoder step** -- the model attends to different parts of the input depending on what it is generating.

## Types of Attention Scoring Functions

### Additive Attention (Bahdanau)

Uses a learned feedforward network:

$$
e_{t,i} = \mathbf{v}^T \tanh(\mathbf{W}_1 \mathbf{h}_t^{\text{dec}} + \mathbf{W}_2 \mathbf{h}_i^{\text{enc}})
$$

where $\mathbf{v}$, $\mathbf{W}_1$, $\mathbf{W}_2$ are learned parameters.

### Dot-Product Attention

Simply computes the dot product:

$$
e_{t,i} = (\mathbf{h}_t^{\text{dec}})^T \mathbf{h}_i^{\text{enc}}
$$

No additional parameters. Fast and efficient, but requires encoder and decoder states to have the same dimension.

### Scaled Dot-Product Attention

Scales by the square root of the dimension to prevent the dot products from growing too large:

$$
e_{t,i} = \frac{(\mathbf{h}_t^{\text{dec}})^T \mathbf{h}_i^{\text{enc}}}{\sqrt{d_k}}
$$

where $d_k$ is the dimension of the key vectors. When $d_k$ is large, the dot products can be very large in magnitude, pushing the softmax into regions with extremely small gradients. The $\frac{1}{\sqrt{d_k}}$ scaling prevents this.

### General Attention (Luong)

Uses a learned weight matrix:

$$
e_{t,i} = (\mathbf{h}_t^{\text{dec}})^T \mathbf{W}_a \mathbf{h}_i^{\text{enc}}
$$

## Query-Key-Value Framework

Attention can be generalized using the **Query-Key-Value (QKV)** abstraction:

- **Query ($\mathbf{Q}$):** What am I looking for? (decoder state)
- **Key ($\mathbf{K}$):** What do I contain? (encoder states, used for matching)
- **Value ($\mathbf{V}$):** What information do I provide? (encoder states, used for the output)

$$
\text{Attention}(\mathbf{Q}, \mathbf{K}, \mathbf{V}) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{K}^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)\mathbf{V}
$$

This formulation is crucial for understanding Transformers (next lessons).

## Worked Example: Attention Weights

Consider an encoder processing "Pick up the red block" with hidden states $\mathbf{h}_1$ through $\mathbf{h}_5$. When the decoder is generating the action "grasp":

- $\alpha_{t,1}$ (Pick) = 0.05
- $\alpha_{t,2}$ (up) = 0.05
- $\alpha_{t,3}$ (the) = 0.02
- $\alpha_{t,4}$ (red) = 0.38
- $\alpha_{t,5}$ (block) = 0.50

The model attends primarily to "red" and "block" -- the parts most relevant to the grasping action. The context vector:

$$
\mathbf{c}_t = 0.05\mathbf{h}_1 + 0.05\mathbf{h}_2 + 0.02\mathbf{h}_3 + 0.38\mathbf{h}_4 + 0.50\mathbf{h}_5
$$

## Benefits of Attention

1. **No information bottleneck:** The decoder can access all encoder states directly
2. **Interpretability:** Attention weights show what the model is focusing on
3. **Better gradient flow:** Provides shortcut connections from decoder back to encoder, helping with vanishing gradients
4. **Handles long sequences:** Performance does not degrade as sequence length increases

## Key Takeaway

> Attention allows the decoder to dynamically focus on different parts of the input at each generation step, eliminating the information bottleneck of vanilla Seq2Seq. The Query-Key-Value framework generalizes attention into a lookup mechanism that will become the foundation of the Transformer architecture.
