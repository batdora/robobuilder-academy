# Recurrent Neural Networks Recap

Standard feedforward networks process each input independently. But many problems in robotics involve **sequences** -- sensor readings over time, language commands, trajectory data. **Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)** are designed to handle sequential data by maintaining a **hidden state** that carries information across time steps.

## Why Sequences Matter

Consider a robot receiving sensor data at each time step. The current observation alone may be ambiguous -- is the object moving left or right? You need **temporal context** to answer that. RNNs provide this by processing inputs one step at a time while maintaining memory.

## RNN Architecture

At each time step $t$, the RNN takes:
- The current input $\mathbf{x}_t$
- The previous hidden state $\mathbf{h}_{t-1}$

And produces:
- A new hidden state $\mathbf{h}_t$
- An output $\mathbf{y}_t$

$$
\mathbf{h}_t = \tanh(\mathbf{W}_{hh}\mathbf{h}_{t-1} + \mathbf{W}_{xh}\mathbf{x}_t + \mathbf{b}_h)
$$

$$
\mathbf{y}_t = \mathbf{W}_{hy}\mathbf{h}_t + \mathbf{b}_y
$$

The **same weight matrices** $\mathbf{W}_{hh}$, $\mathbf{W}_{xh}$, and $\mathbf{W}_{hy}$ are shared across all time steps. This is called **weight sharing** or **parameter tying**.

## Unrolling Through Time

To understand how RNNs process a sequence, we "unroll" the network across time steps:

$$
\mathbf{h}_0 \xrightarrow{\mathbf{x}_1} \mathbf{h}_1 \xrightarrow{\mathbf{x}_2} \mathbf{h}_2 \xrightarrow{\mathbf{x}_3} \cdots \xrightarrow{\mathbf{x}_T} \mathbf{h}_T
$$

Each hidden state $\mathbf{h}_t$ is a function of the **entire history** of inputs $\mathbf{x}_1, \mathbf{x}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{x}_t$. The hidden state acts as a compressed summary of everything seen so far.

## Backpropagation Through Time (BPTT)

Training RNNs uses **Backpropagation Through Time (BPTT)**. We unroll the network for $T$ time steps and compute gradients by applying the chain rule backwards through time:

$$
\frac{\partial J}{\partial \mathbf{W}_{hh}} = \sum_{t=1}^{T} \frac{\partial J_t}{\partial \mathbf{W}_{hh}}
$$

Each $\frac{\partial J_t}{\partial \mathbf{W}_{hh}}$ requires computing products of Jacobians:

$$
\frac{\partial \mathbf{h}_t}{\partial \mathbf{h}_k} = \prod_{i=k+1}^{t} \frac{\partial \mathbf{h}_i}{\partial \mathbf{h}_{i-1}}
$$

## The Vanishing and Exploding Gradient Problem

This product of Jacobians is where things go wrong. Each factor $\frac{\partial \mathbf{h}_i}{\partial \mathbf{h}_{i-1}}$ involves the weight matrix $\mathbf{W}_{hh}$ and the derivative of $\tanh$.

- If the **spectral radius** of $\mathbf{W}_{hh}$ is less than 1: gradients **vanish** exponentially -- the network cannot learn long-range dependencies
- If the **spectral radius** of $\mathbf{W}_{hh}$ is greater than 1: gradients **explode** -- the training becomes unstable

$$
\left\| \prod_{i=k+1}^{t} \frac{\partial \mathbf{h}_i}{\partial \mathbf{h}_{i-1}} \right\| \leq \left( \| \mathbf{W}_{hh} \| \cdot \gamma \right)^{t-k}
$$

where $\gamma$ is the bound on the $\tanh$ derivative (at most 1).

## Gradient Clipping

A practical fix for **exploding gradients** is **gradient clipping**:

$$
\hat{\mathbf{g}} = \begin{cases} \frac{\tau}{\|\mathbf{g}\|} \mathbf{g} & \text{if } \|\mathbf{g}\| > \tau \\ \mathbf{g} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
$$

where $\tau$ is a threshold. This rescales the gradient when its norm exceeds $\tau$, preventing parameter updates from being too large.

## RNN Variants by Output Structure

Depending on the task, RNNs can be configured as:

| Pattern | Input | Output | Example |
|---------|-------|--------|---------|
| **Many-to-one** | Sequence | Single vector | Sentiment analysis |
| **One-to-many** | Single vector | Sequence | Image captioning |
| **Many-to-many** | Sequence | Sequence (same length) | POS tagging |
| **Many-to-many** | Sequence | Sequence (different length) | Translation |

## Worked Example: Hidden State Computation

Given $\mathbf{W}_{xh} = [1, 0.5]$, $\mathbf{W}_{hh} = [0.8]$, $\mathbf{b}_h = [0]$, $\mathbf{h}_0 = [0]$, and inputs $x_1 = 1$, $x_2 = 0.5$:

**Step 1:** $\mathbf{h}_1 = \tanh(0.8 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot 1 + 0) = \tanh(1) \approx 0.762$

**Step 2:** $\mathbf{h}_2 = \tanh(0.8 \cdot 0.762 + 0.5 \cdot 0.5 + 0) = \tanh(0.860) \approx 0.696$

Notice how $\mathbf{h}_2$ depends on both $x_2$ and the information from $x_1$ carried through $\mathbf{h}_1$.

## Key Takeaway

> RNNs process sequences by maintaining a hidden state that summarizes past information. However, vanilla RNNs struggle with long-range dependencies due to vanishing gradients during BPTT. This fundamental limitation motivates the gated architectures we will study next.
